About Me

Nina Koroleva

ART ARTIST / DESIGNER

Nina Koroleva, born in Tomsk, Russia, in 1979, has been passionate about art since childhood. Her journey began with her graduation with honors from Tomsk's Children's Art School in 1995. Over the years, Nina explored various creative forms, from crafting exquisite handmade jewelry and painting vibrant stained glass designs to creating bespoke interior pieces from epoxy resin and hand-painting delicate tableware and crystal. With every new endeavor, she brings her artistic vision to life, turning the ordinary into the extraordinary, always driven by a deep love for beauty and craftsmanship.

Experience

In 2002, Nina Koroleva moved to St. Petersburg, where she lived and worked for many years. During her time there, she took private lessons in oil painting from renowned artist Vladimir Zenin, a member of the Russian Artists' Union. In 2021, Nina relocated to Israel and, in 2022, became a member of the Artists' Union of Netanya.

After moving to Israel, Nina's artistic style underwent a significant transformation. What once leaned toward dark, depressive abstractionism has now blossomed into vibrant cubism-suprematism. Her use of bright, contrasting colors reflects joy, light, and positivity, making her work a perfect complement to both home and office interiors, highlighting the beauty of any space.
